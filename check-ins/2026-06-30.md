**New concept — Bytes vs Bits**
- **bit = a single brick; byte = a pallet of 8 bricks.** The link counts bricks per second (bits); files are measured in pallets (bytes). The factor between them is **8**.
- The marketing trap: ISPs always advertise in **Mbps** (number is 8× bigger); downloads show **MB/s**. A "500 mega" plan downloads at `500 ÷ 8 = 62.5 MB/s` — same internet, two languages.
- Golden rule: **check the units match before dividing.** Full bilingual note with production flashcards (PT→EN) added to the vault.
