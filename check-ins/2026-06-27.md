**24h retest — Lab 2.4 (passed, core 3/3)**
- **Fatual (MTU):** correct, with a unit fix — MTU is **1500 bytes**, not bits (the fragmentation math only closes in bytes). MTU is a property of the **link**, not the machine.
- **Relation (DF on vs off):** correct — DF on = no fragment, drop + ICMP "Fragmentation Needed" to the source; DF off = fragment + reassemble at destination.
- **Application (lost fragment):** core correct (one lost piece → the whole packet dies). Fixed a detail: plain fragmentation that loses a piece is a **silent loss** — the source does **not** get a helpful ICMP. That helpful ICMP is exactly what **DF + Path MTU Discovery** provides.

**Deepening (learner-driven)**
- **Path MTU Discovery:** the source marks DF, gets ICMP notes with the next-hop MTU, shrinks, and retries until it converges on the smallest MTU of the whole path. Needs DF — without it the router just fragments silently and no note returns.
- **Who shrinks the packet:** the kernel, automatically (per-destination route cache). With TCP it lowers the **MSS** and retransmits the dropped packet smaller on its own; UDP leaves it to the app. → the **PMTUD black hole** (a firewall eating the ICMP → connects but stalls on large transfers).
- **Encapsulation:** box-inside-a-box — the IP packet's payload is a TCP segment, whose payload is a slice of real data (a YouTube video chunk). Tied back to the artificial `-s 2000` filler vs real useful payload.
